## Terms

A **term** is a phrase you can use to position your product. It is the kind of language you might use in a landing page headline, tagline, meta title, or product description.

Terms describe a category or market people actually search for.

| Good terms | Why |
|---|---|
| "waterproof hiking shoes" | Specific product category |
| "invoice software for freelancers" | Clear market + audience |
| "SOC 2 compliance software" | Defined category with search demand |

| Not terms | Why |
|---|---|
| "shoes" | Too broad |
| "How to choose waterproof hiking shoes" | This is an article idea, not a term |
| "best waterproof hiking shoes for wide feet" | This is a variation of the main term, not a separate term |

RankEarly groups close variations under the same term. "Best AI content writer" and "top AI content writer" both roll up to **"AI content writer"** instead of showing up as two different terms.

## Pillars and subtopics

A **pillar** is a top-level content planning unit that groups related subtopics under one subject.

- A pillar has a **pillar name** and a **subject**
- A pillar contains multiple **subtopics**
- A pillar helps you organize a broader content area, not one single article

A **subtopic** is an article-sized content opportunity inside that pillar. In RankEarly, **subtopic** and **keyword cluster** mean the same thing.

- It groups related keywords that point to the same search intent
- It usually maps to one content piece you could brief and write
- You can still inspect the keywords behind it when you want more detail

## Keyword libraries

A **keyword library** is where RankEarly keeps the keywords and saved search results for a project.

- [Keyword Expansion](/research/keyword-expansion) adds more keywords to it
- [Primary terms](/research/primary-term) uses it to find terms
- [Topic clusters](/research/topic-clusters) uses it to build pillars and subtopics

This is why Keyword Expansion is usually the first step for a new project. It gives RankEarly more search data to work with later.

## Terms and subtopics

Terms and subtopics solve different problems.

| | Terms | Subtopics |
|---|---|---|
| **What** | Positioning phrases | Content pieces to write |
| **Example** | "waterproof hiking shoes" | "How to choose waterproof hiking shoes" |
| **Used for** | Landing pages, taglines, meta titles | Blog posts, guides, articles |
| **Found via** | Primary terms | Topic clusters |

In practice:

- **Terms** help you decide the market language you want to own
- **Subtopics** help you decide the articles or pages you should publish
- **Pillars** help you organize many subtopics into one broader content plan

## Winnability

RankEarly uses winnability to tell you whether an opportunity is worth targeting given the current competitive landscape.

At the keyword level, RankEarly looks at the search results and decides whether the keyword looks **Winnable** or not. The full breakdown lives in [SERP gap analysis](/how-it-works/serp-gap-analysis).

Across the product, you will usually see three labels:

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Winnable** | There is a realistic opening here |
| **Challenging** | Some signals look good, but competition is mixed |
| **Skip** | RankEarly does not see a good opening right now |

[Winnability](/how-it-works/winnability) explains how those labels are applied in each workflow.

## Research modes

RankEarly offers four research modes plus one downstream writing workflow.

| Workflow | Question it answers | Output |
|---|---|---|
| **Keyword Expansion** | "What else do people search for around this idea?" | 100 to 500 keywords saved to your project |
| **Primary terms** | "Which positioning terms can I realistically target?" | Ranked terms with support queries and verdicts |
| **Topic clusters** | "How should I turn these keywords into a content plan?" | Pillars plus subtopics / keyword clusters |
| **Content Angles** | "How should I approach this one idea?" | 5 content angles with verdicts |
| **Content Brief** | "How do I turn a chosen keyword into a writing brief?" | A step-by-step brief with intent, format, must-includes, title, and outline |

Keyword Expansion is usually the first step when you are starting from an idea and want more search data to work from. [Primary terms](/research/primary-term) and [Topic clusters](/research/topic-clusters) are strongest after that. [Content Angles](/research/content-angles) can be used any time you already have one idea and need better framing.

- **Starting from scratch?** Run Keyword Expansion first, then move into Topic Clusters or Primary terms depending on whether you want content planning or positioning.
- **Have an idea and need framing?** Run Content Angles when you want to explore how to approach one specific article idea.
- **Ready to write?** Launch Content Brief from a validated keyword, support query, or subtopic.

## Projects

A project is where all your research lives. It stores your business context, such as product description, target audience, competitors, business goals, and seed phrases.

Inside a project:

- **Research History** keeps past runs so you can reopen or compare them later
- **Saved Items** lets you bookmark specific terms, angles, and pillars that you want to keep working from
- **Keyword libraries** keep the keywords and search results that later workflows use
