## Terms

A **term** is a semantically distinct phrase you can use for positioning and product communication — landing page headlines, taglines, meta titles, ad copy.

Terms represent a category or market and have real search demand.

| Good terms | Why |
|---|---|
| "waterproof hiking shoes" | Specific product category |
| "invoice software for freelancers" | Clear market + audience |
| "SOC 2 compliance software" | Defined category with search demand |

| Not terms | Why |
|---|---|
| "shoes" | Too broad |
| "How to choose waterproof hiking shoes" | This is a topic, not a term |
| "best waterproof hiking shoes for wide feet" | Modifier variation — belongs to the "waterproof hiking shoes" term |

RankEarly groups modifier variations under the same term. "Best AI content writer" and "top AI content writer" both belong to the **"AI content writer"** term. This prevents false positives where variations appear as separate candidates.

## Topics

A **topic** is an intent-driven content idea — not just a keyword, but a specific piece of content you should write.

```
Topic: "How to Choose Waterproof Hiking Shoes"
├── Primary keyword: waterproof hiking shoes
├── Related keywords: how to choose hiking shoes, best waterproof shoes
├── Intent: Informational
└── Winnability: Build / Skip
```

Traditional SEO tools show **keywords to rank for**, which requires expertise to turn into content ideas. RankEarly shows **content pieces to write** — directly actionable even without SEO background.

### Terms vs topics

| | Terms | Topics |
|---|---|---|
| **What** | Positioning phrases | Content pieces to write |
| **Example** | "waterproof hiking shoes" | "How to Choose Waterproof Hiking Shoes" |
| **Used for** | Landing pages, taglines, meta titles | Blog posts, guides, articles |
| **Found via** | Primary Term research | Topic Clusters, Content Angles |

## Winnability

Every term and topic in RankEarly gets a winnability verdict. This tells you whether it's worth targeting given the current competitive landscape.

### How it works

RankEarly runs a **SERP gap analysis** on each keyword. Instead of relying only on keyword difficulty scores, it uses AI to evaluate six signals from the top 10 search results:

| Signal | Good sign | Bad sign |
|---|---|---|
| Small sites or forums ranking | Smaller sites can compete | Only big brands dominate |
| Mixed search intent | Google isn't sure — you can wedge in | Clear standard to beat |
| Content format gap | Users want something results don't provide | Results already match |
| Low-quality results | Thin or repetitive content — quality gap | Results are solid |
| Strong competitors | No authority barriers | Official docs, .gov, giants |
| Click-stealing SERP features | Organic results get clicks | Ads and panels push you down |

A keyword is **Winnable** when it has 3+ positive signals and at most 1 negative signal. Otherwise, it's **Skip**.

### Verdicts

Different research modes aggregate keyword-level results into higher-level verdicts.

**Primary Term** counts how many queries (the term itself + its support queries) are winnable:

| Verdict | Condition | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Build** | 3+ winnable queries | Clear opportunity — multiple angles reinforce the term |
| **Challenging** | 1-2 winnable queries | Mixed signals, some angles viable |
| **Skip** | 0 winnable queries | No winnable queries in this cluster |

**Topic Clusters** and **Content Angles** apply winnability at the topic level, evaluating whether the keyword cluster behind each topic has enough opportunity to justify writing it.

## Research modes

RankEarly offers four research modes. Each answers a different question and can be used independently or together.

| Mode | Question it answers | Output |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary Term** | "Which terms can I rank for?" | Up to 10 terms with winnability verdicts |
| **Topic Clusters** | "What should I write about?" | Up to 10 pillars, each with subtopic clusters |
| **Content Angles** | "How should I write about this?" | 5 content angles for a given idea |
| **Keyword Expansion** | "What keywords exist for this idea?" | 100-500 keywords for bulk research |

Keyword Expansion is usually the first step when you're starting from an idea and want a reusable keyword library. Other modes can still be run independently, but [Topic Clusters](/research/topic-clusters) and [Primary Term](/research/primary-term) are strongest when that library is already populated.

- **Starting from scratch?** Run Keyword Expansion first, then move into Topic Clusters or Primary Term depending on whether you want content planning or positioning.
- **Have an idea and need framing?** Run Content Angles when you want to explore how to approach one specific topic.
- **Strategy evolving?** Re-run any mode as your market, product, or priorities change.

## Projects

A project is the container for all your research. It stores your business context — product description, target audience, competitors, seed phrases — and every research mode uses it as input.

You can run any research mode multiple times within a project. Past results are saved in **Research History**, and you can bookmark specific findings to **Saved Items** for quick reference.
