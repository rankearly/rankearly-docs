## Keyword

A word or phrase that people type into a search engine. "Email deliverability" is a keyword. So is "how to improve email open rates." RankEarly tracks keywords so you can see what people are searching for and how hard it is to rank for each one.

## Keyword Cluster

A group of keywords that mean roughly the same thing or that Google treats as the same topic. For example, "email deliverability tips," "how to improve email delivery," and "why emails go to spam" would likely belong to one cluster — because Google shows similar results for all of them.

RankEarly groups keywords into clusters automatically so you don't waste time writing separate posts for queries that Google already considers the same topic.

## Topic Cluster

A broader content strategy structure. A topic cluster is a set of keyword clusters that all relate to the same subject area. For example, "email deliverability" as a topic might include keyword clusters around authentication (SPF, DKIM), sender reputation, list hygiene, and inbox placement.

In practice, you'd build a topic cluster by creating a pillar page for the broad subject and linking it to individual blog posts that cover each keyword cluster in depth.

## Pillar Page

A page that covers a broad topic at a high level and links out to more detailed posts on subtopics. It acts as the hub of a topic cluster. For example, a pillar page on "Email Deliverability" would summarize authentication, sender reputation, and list hygiene — then link to dedicated blog posts that go deep on each.

## Information Gain

The unique value your content adds beyond what already ranks on page one. If every top result explains what SPF records are but none explain how to debug a failing SPF check, that debugging angle is your information gain.

RankEarly's `/content-researcher` skill finds these gaps by reading the top-ranking pages and surfacing questions they don't answer well.

## SERP

Search Engine Results Page — the page Google shows after you search for something. When we say "analyze the SERP," we mean looking at what's currently ranking for a given keyword: which pages appear, what they cover, and where the gaps are.

## Topical Authority

The idea that search engines trust sites that cover a subject thoroughly. A site with 15 well-linked posts on email deliverability signals more expertise than a site with one general post. Building topical authority is the main reason to create content groups rather than standalone posts.

## Under-Discussed Questions

Questions that searchers likely have but that the top-ranking pages don't answer well (or at all). These are surfaced by RankEarly's `/content-researcher` skill and represent opportunities — topics where your content can provide something new that existing results don't.
