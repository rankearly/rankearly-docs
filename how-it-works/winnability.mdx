Winnability is how RankEarly answers the question: "Can I actually rank for this?" The underlying check starts at the keyword level by looking at the current Google results and asking whether the SERP shows enough real openings to justify pursuing.

At the keyword level, the verdict is binary: **Winnable** or **Hard to Win**. Higher-level workflows like [Primary Term](/research/primary-term) and [Content Angles](/research/content-angles) aggregate these keyword verdicts into three-tier labels (Winnable / Challenging / Hard to Win) based on how many support keywords are winnable.

## Keyword-level signals

At the keyword level, RankEarly reviews the top Google results and checks seven signals.

### Positive signals

These are signals that suggest a newer or lower-authority site may still have a realistic opening:

| Signal | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| **Small sites, forums, or UGC in top 10** | Google is already allowing lower-authority pages to rank |
| **Stale pages ranking high** | Fresher content may be enough to compete |
| **Mixed search intent** | Google is not fully settled on one interpretation of the query |
| **Content format gap** | Searchers may want a format the current results do not provide |
| **Thin or generic content** | A stronger, more specific page can stand out |

### Barrier signals

These are signals that make ranking materially harder:

| Signal | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| **High-authority domains dominate** | The SERP is controlled by brands, official docs, or other hard-to-displace sites |
| **High keyword difficulty** | The keyword metric itself suggests a tougher ranking environment |

## Verdict rule

The snippet-based keyword check is intentionally simple:

- **Winnable**: 3 or more positive signals and at most 1 barrier
- **Hard to Win**: does not meet that threshold

This is a quick screen, not a full content brief. It tells you whether the live SERP looks open enough to investigate further.

## Choosing what to target

Here's a practical framework for using verdicts across research modes:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Start with Winnable verdicts">
    **Winnable** results have the clearest path to ranking. For new sites especially, these are your best first targets.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Hard to Win doesn't mean impossible">
    A **Hard to Win** verdict means the SERP doesn't show a clear opening right now. That does not mean the keyword is permanently off-limits. Competition changes over time, so it can be worth checking again later.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Higher-level rollups add nuance">
    Workflows like [Primary Term](/research/primary-term) and [Content Angles](/research/content-angles) aggregate multiple keyword verdicts into a three-tier label: **Winnable** (3+ winnable keywords), **Challenging** (1-2 winnable), or **Hard to Win** (none winnable). This helps you prioritize at a glance.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Combine modes for a full picture">
    Use [Primary Term](/research/primary-term) to explore positioning terms, [Topic Clusters](/research/topic-clusters) to plan what to write, and [Content Brief](/planning/content-brief) when you are ready to turn a chosen keyword into a draft-ready brief.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
