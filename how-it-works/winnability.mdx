Winnability is how RankEarly answers the question: "Can I actually rank for this?" Across the product, you will see three labels: **Winnable**, **Challenging**, and **Skip**.

## Keyword-level: Winnable or Skip

At the keyword level, [SERP gap analysis](/how-it-works/serp-gap-analysis) evaluates 6 signals from the top 10 Google results and produces a binary verdict:

- **Winnable**: 3+ opportunity signals and at most 1 barrier
- **Skip**: Does not meet the threshold

This per-keyword verdict is the building block. Research modes aggregate multiple keyword verdicts into higher-level decisions.

## Primary Term verdicts

Each candidate term has up to 6 queries: the term itself plus up to 5 support queries. The term verdict counts how many of these are winnable.

| Verdict | Condition | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| **Winnable** | 3+ queries winnable | Multiple angles support this term, so the opportunity looks strong |
| **Challenging** | 1-2 queries winnable | Some angles work, but the signal is mixed |
| **Skip** | 0 queries winnable | No winnable queries, so competition looks too strong right now |

**How terms are ranked:**

1. Sort by total winnable queries (highest first), then by search volume
2. The top **Winnable** term is recommended as your primary term
3. Other **Winnable** terms are shown as alternatives
4. **Challenging** and **Skip** terms are available in the full list

<Tip>
A **Challenging** term is not necessarily bad. If the term is central to your product, it may still be worth targeting over time, especially if one of its support queries looks strong.
</Tip>

## Content Angles verdicts

Each content angle has 6 keyword variations. The final verdict counts how many of those keywords are winnable.

| Verdict | Condition | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| **Winnable** | 3+ keywords winnable | Most phrasings of this angle look promising |
| **Challenging** | 1-2 keywords winnable | Some phrasings work, but competition is mixed |
| **Skip** | 0 keywords winnable | None of the phrasings look like a good target right now |

The results page shows all 5 angles with their verdicts. Pick the angle that combines the best winnability with the most relevance to your audience.

## Topic Clusters verdicts

Topic Clusters works differently from Primary Term and Content Angles. Instead of running SERP gap analysis on every keyword, it uses keyword difficulty (KD) and search volume as primary metrics for subtopics.

Each subtopic cluster shows:
- **Keywords** in the cluster
- **Aggregated search volume** across all keywords
- **Average KD** across the cluster

Lower KD and higher volume clusters are your best bets. Use these metrics to prioritize which subtopics to write first.

For deeper validation, you can create a [Content Brief](/planning/content-brief) from any subtopic, which runs full SERP scraping and intent analysis.

## Choosing what to target

Here's a practical framework for using verdicts across research modes:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Start with Winnable verdicts">
    **Winnable** results have the clearest path to ranking. For new sites especially, these are your best first targets.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Don't write off Challenging">
    **Challenging** means some signals are positive. If a term or angle is highly relevant to your product, it can still pay off as your site gains authority from earlier wins.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Skip means skip (for now)">
    A **Skip** verdict means no winnable queries were found. That does not mean the keyword is permanently off-limits. Competition changes over time, so it can be worth checking again later.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Combine modes for a full picture">
    Use [Primary Term](/research/primary-term) to find your positioning terms, then [Topic Clusters](/research/topic-clusters) to plan content around them. If one article idea stands out, run [Content Angles](/research/content-angles) to find the best approach.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
