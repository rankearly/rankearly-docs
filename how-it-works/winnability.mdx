Winnability is how RankEarly answers the question: "Can I actually rank for this?" Every term, topic, and pillar gets a verdict — **Build**, **Challenging**, or **Skip** — so you can focus your effort on real opportunities.

## Keyword-level: Winnable or Skip

At the keyword level, [SERP gap analysis](/how-it-works/serp-gap-analysis) evaluates 6 signals from the top 10 Google results and produces a binary verdict:

- **Winnable** — 3+ opportunity signals and at most 1 barrier
- **Skip** — doesn't meet the threshold

This per-keyword verdict is the building block. Research modes aggregate multiple keyword verdicts into higher-level decisions.

## Primary Term verdicts

Each candidate term has up to 6 queries: the term itself plus up to 5 support queries. The term verdict counts how many of these are winnable.

| Verdict | Condition | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| **Build** | 3+ queries winnable | Multiple angles support this term — strong opportunity |
| **Challenging** | 1-2 queries winnable | Some angles work, but the signal is mixed |
| **Skip** | 0 queries winnable | No winnable queries — competition is too strong across the board |

**How terms are ranked:**

1. Sort by total winnable queries (highest first), then by search volume
2. The top **Build** term is recommended as your primary term
3. Other **Build** terms are shown as alternatives
4. **Challenging** and **Skip** terms are available in the full list

<Tip>
A **Challenging** term isn't necessarily bad. If the term is central to your product, it might be worth targeting with a long-term strategy — especially if one of its support queries has strong winnability.
</Tip>

## Content Angles verdicts

Each content angle has 6 keyword variations. The topic verdict counts how many keywords are winnable.

| Verdict | Condition | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| **Build** | 3+ keywords winnable | Strong opportunity — most phrasings of this angle are winnable |
| **Challenging** | 1-2 keywords winnable | Mixed — some phrasings work, others face stiff competition |
| **Skip** | 0 keywords winnable | Weak across the board — all phrasings are too competitive |

The results page shows all 5 angles with their verdicts. Pick the angle that combines the best winnability with the most relevance to your audience.

## Topic Clusters verdicts

Topic Clusters works differently from Primary Term and Content Angles. Instead of running SERP gap analysis on every keyword, it uses keyword difficulty (KD) and search volume as primary metrics for subtopics.

Each subtopic cluster shows:
- **Keywords** in the cluster
- **Aggregated search volume** across all keywords
- **Average KD** across the cluster

Lower KD and higher volume clusters are your best bets. Use these metrics to prioritize which subtopics to write first.

For deeper validation, you can create a [Content Brief](/planning/content-brief) from any subtopic, which runs full SERP scraping and intent analysis.

## Choosing what to target

Here's a practical framework for using verdicts across research modes:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Start with Build verdicts">
    **Build** results have the clearest path to ranking. For new sites especially, these are your highest-ROI targets. Start here to build early momentum and domain authority.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Don't write off Challenging">
    **Challenging** means some signals are positive. If a term or angle is highly relevant to your product, it can still pay off — particularly as your site gains authority from your **Build** wins.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Skip means skip (for now)">
    A **Skip** verdict means no winnable queries were found. This doesn't mean the keyword is permanently off-limits — competition changes over time. Revisit skipped results in a few months as your site grows.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Combine modes for a full picture">
    Use [Primary Term](/research/primary-term) to find your positioning terms, then [Topic Clusters](/research/topic-clusters) to plan content around them. If a specific topic interests you, run [Content Angles](/research/content-angles) to find the best approach. Each mode validates a different piece of your strategy.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
