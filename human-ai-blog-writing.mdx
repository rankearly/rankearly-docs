Share what you know about your business, and we deliver finished articles ready to publish — written for both search engines and AI answer engines. See [pricing](https://www.rankearly.pro/pricing) for current packs.

Each article goes through a seven-step research, writing, and editorial process. We cover what the top-ranking posts on your topic already cover, then go deeper on the angles they skip. A content writer on our team signs off before anything reaches you.

<Note>
  Good content depends on good context. If we misunderstand your product, audience, or offer, the article may miss the mark even when the writing quality is high.
</Note>

## When to use it

The service is a good fit when:

- Your site is new and you need real impressions to learn which keywords are worth targeting. It's more useful to ship a handful of articles and learn from the data than to spend days polishing one post.
- Your team is small and most of your time goes into product, launch, sales, and customer work. You want writing done without hiring a full-time content person.
- You have useful business insights but prefer not to write long SEO articles yourself. You can describe what you know and let us convert it into search-ready posts.

If you prefer to run the research and writing workflow yourself, start with the [Quickstart](/quickstart) and use the self-serve platform.

## How we write each article

Every article we write follows the same seven-step process. Here's what happens at each stage.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Map the SERP and AI answers">
    We start by reading what's already ranking — and what AI answer engines surface for the query. What format works best for this topic — a list, a how-to guide, a comparison? What questions do all the top results answer? More importantly, what are they missing? Those gaps are where new articles earn their place, and they represent information readers want. This aligns with Google's <a href="https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/creating-helpful-content">people-first content</a> guidance.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Research the under-discussed angles">
    Once we identify the gaps, we research thoroughly to find substantive answers. We prioritize specific solutions over generic overviews that dominate search results. The detailed, useful answers are what people cite, share, and link to — and what AI engines pull into their answers — so that's what we write.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Keep the article focused">
    Research always uncovers more than fits in one piece. We're selective about what stays. Even if something's interesting, if it drifts from the core topic, we remove it. The article ends up delivering what the title promises — which is what readers expect and search engines reward.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify solutions still work">
    Information changes frequently. Screenshots from months ago often don't match the current interface. This is where how-to content becomes outdated: the steps worked when published, but no longer apply. Before including anything in your article, we verify that all information is current and accurate. Outdated guidance is worse than no article at all.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Integrate your business">
    We incorporate the business context you've approved: your methodology, offerings, and what makes your business different. These unique details strengthen the article. Only you have this information, so other articles on the same topic cannot include it. This distinguishes your content from generic competitors.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run a CORE-EEAT check">
    Before the article reaches editing, it goes through a CORE-EEAT review. Are sources real and credible? Is authorship clear? Does the piece demonstrate first-hand experience rather than rehashing what others said? Is it structured so search engines and AI can extract information clearly? If any element needs improvement, we address it before proceeding.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Editorial review">
    Final step: a content writer reviews the complete article. They remove repetition, refine phrasing, and ensure smooth flow. This is the final checkpoint — if the piece isn't ready, it doesn't go out. We prioritize quality over speed, and wouldn't send anything we wouldn't publish ourselves.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What we need from you

First, we need the website you're looking to improve — the one where you want better search visibility, clearer positioning, or more conversions from content.

We read through your site, check the sitemap, and gather available public information. Then we write up our understanding of your business and what the articles should focus on. Before any writing starts, we send those notes for your review: **the business context, glossary, and topic ideas we're planning to use**. This is where you correct any misunderstandings.

The most important part is the business context:

- What you sell
- Who you serve
- What problems you solve
- What makes you different from alternatives
- Product details, customer language, or proof points we should use
- The vocabulary, brand voice, and wording you prefer

Once you confirm it, this context becomes the glossary we write every article against. Your vocabulary and phrasing stay consistent across posts, instead of getting replaced with generic SEO copy.

### If your website is thin

If your site is new or has limited content, we need additional reference material before writing begins. You have three options.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a public reference page (recommended)">
    Add user-facing documentation or reference pages that explain your product, features, services, use cases, and positioning. These pages help us understand your business, and they provide additional content for search engines to index.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share internal docs">
    Send internal notes, sales docs, product documentation, onboarding material, or customer research (remove confidential data before sharing). We save the relevant information as context to identify search opportunities where your product or service can help. Without that context, we may miss strong matches.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write the context yourself">
    Edit the context document directly. You control what to share, what to describe, and how your offer should be framed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Public reference pages help with topical authority as well. They sometimes attract search queries you weren't targeting, and we can use those signals to plan better topics and convert that search intent into stronger SEO and GEO content.

The more complete your references, the less likely we'll overlook product details, positioning, or audience needs. Once the context is approved, we write following that guidance.

## Communication

Slack works well for faster feedback, especially when confirming context or handling revisions. Email is also acceptable if you prefer that channel.

Timely replies keep work moving efficiently. The most important responses:

- Corrections to business context
- Approvals or changes to topic choices
- Feedback on writing style
- Flagging missing facts before we start revisions

## Revisions

We include three free revision rounds if key context was missing or misunderstood. When that occurs, we often rebuild from the outline up, then run another internal review before sending it back.

For small wording preferences or stylistic choices, editing the article yourself is usually faster — and gives you more control over the final voice.

We appreciate style feedback. Once you share preferences, we apply them to future articles.

## Refunds

If you're unsatisfied with the service and wish to stop, you can request a refund for the remaining undelivered posts. You only pay for the posts that have been delivered.

For example, if you've received 3 posts out of a 10-post pack and decide to discontinue, we refund 70% of the pack price — the cost of the 7 remaining posts.

## What it's not for

The service is focused on blog content. It doesn't replace rank tracking, backlink work, technical SEO audits, or a full agency retainer.
