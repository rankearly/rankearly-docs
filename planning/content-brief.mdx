Content Brief analyzes the top Google results for a keyword cluster and generates everything you need to write a competitive article. You get search intent analysis, must-include topics, format recommendations, title options, and a full outline.

## When to use it

- You've identified a keyword cluster from [Topic Clusters](/research/topic-clusters) and want to write about it
- You need to understand what Google rewards for a specific keyword before writing
- You want a structured brief to hand off to a writer or use as an AI writing prompt

## Entry point

Content Brief is accessible from two places:

1. **Topic Clusters research page** — after running a Topic Clusters research
2. **Pillars page** — from your saved pillars

Open the menu and select **Content Brief**, then pick a pillar and keyword cluster.

## How it works

The process has two phases: an automatic analysis that runs immediately, and a guided walkthrough you step through at your own pace.

### Phase 1: Automatic analysis

When you create a content brief, RankEarly immediately:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Scrapes top results">
    Fetches the top 10 Google results for your primary keyword, filters out non-article pages (Reddit, Quora, PDFs, login walls), and scrapes the remaining pages for their content, heading structure, and word counts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Classifies search intent">
    Identifies 1 dominant intent and up to 2 secondary intents from the scraped pages. Each intent gets a label, description, and the specific pages that match it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Extracts must-include items">
    For each intent, extracts 5 topics or points that appear across the top results and would be noticeably absent if you skipped them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Analyzes word counts">
    Computes word counts for each scraped page and recommends a target range based on the most common length among top results.
  </Step>
</Steps>

You'll see the results immediately: intent cards with grouped pages, must-include items, and a word count distribution chart.

### Phase 2: Guided walkthrough

Click **Generate Content Brief** to open a step-by-step dialog that builds your brief:

| Step | What you do |
|---|---|
| 1. Select intent | Choose the dominant or a secondary intent to target |
| 2. Must-include items | Review the 5 must-include topics — disable any that don't fit |
| 3. Information gain | Review how your product fills the content gap — edit or regenerate |
| 4. Content format | Pick from 2-5 detected formats (e.g., how-to guide, listicle, comparison) |
| 5. Word count | Select a target word count range |
| 6. Title | Choose from 5 generated title suggestions |
| 7. Outline | Review the generated H2/H3 outline with section descriptions |

Each step auto-saves after you confirm. If you leave and come back, the walkthrough picks up where you left off.

## What you get

The final content brief includes:

- **Target intent** with description
- **Must-include items** — topics your article should cover
- **Information gain** — how your product adds unique value
- **Content format** — the structure that works for this keyword
- **Target word count** — based on what's ranking
- **Title** — selected from AI-generated options
- **H2/H3 outline** — section headings with descriptions

You can copy the full brief as Markdown to use directly as a writing prompt or hand off to a writer.

## Tips

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Information gain is your differentiator">
    The information gain section connects your product to the content. It explains how your tool, solution, or expertise fills a gap that existing results don't cover. Edit this to make it specific — generic information gain leads to generic articles.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Use the brief as an AI prompt">
    The Markdown export is designed to work as a writing prompt for AI tools. Copy it, paste it into your preferred AI writing tool, and you'll get a first draft that matches what Google rewards for the keyword.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Don't skip must-include items lightly">
    Must-include items represent topics that appear across multiple top results. Skipping them means your article will be missing something searchers expect. Only disable items that genuinely don't apply to your angle.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
