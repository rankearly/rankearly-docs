Content Brief helps you turn a keyword into a clear content brief grounded in what already ranks. It surfaces search intent, likely content format, must-cover topics, title directions, and an outline so you can brief a writer or move straight into drafting.

## When to use it

- You've picked a subtopic from [Topic Clustering](/research/topic-clustering) and want to turn it into a content brief
- You already know the keyword you want to target
- You need to understand what Google rewards for a specific keyword before writing
- You want a structured brief to hand off to a writer or use as an AI writing prompt

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Analyze what already ranks">
    RankEarly reviews the current ranking pages for your keyword to understand what searchers expect and what Google is rewarding right now.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Extract the patterns that matter">
    It identifies the dominant intent, common content format, recurring topics, and a realistic content depth based on the pages in the SERP.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the direction and complete the brief">
    You choose among the recommended intents, formats, must-include topics, word-count ranges, and title directions, define the article's information gain, and get an outline you can use for drafting.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What you get

The final content brief includes:

- **Target intent** with description
- **Content format** that matches the ranking pages
- **Must-include items** - topics your article should cover
- **Information gain** - how your product adds unique value
- **Target word count** - based on what's ranking
- **Title** - a recommended direction for the article
- **Outline** - section headings with descriptions
- **Markdown brief** - ready to use in your writing workflow

You can use the Markdown export directly as a prompt for AI writing agents or hand it off to a content writer.

## Tips

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Information gain is your differentiator">
    The information gain section is where you connect the brief to your product, experience, or point of view. Make it specific enough that the article adds something the existing results do not.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Use the Markdown brief as your prompt">
    The Markdown export is a good starting point for drafting because it already packages the intent, format, topics, information gain, and outline in one place.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Treat must-include topics as strong signals">
    Must-include topics usually reflect what appears across multiple ranking pages. Change them when your angle requires it, but do it deliberately.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
