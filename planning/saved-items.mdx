Saved Items lets you bookmark specific findings from across your research sessions. While [Research History](#research-history) keeps a record of every research you've run, Saved Items gives you a curated collection of the results that matter most.

## What you can save

| Item type | Saved from | What's stored |
|---|---|---|
| **Terms** | [Primary Term](/research/primary-term) research | Term name, winnability verdict, search volume, difficulty |
| **Pillars** | [Topic Clusters](/research/topic-clusters) research | Pillar name, subject, full keyword cluster data |

## How to save

During any research, click the **save button** on individual results you want to keep. Saved items appear in the **Saved** section of the respective dashboard page (Terms or Pillars).

## How to use saved items

Each saved item card shows:

- Title or name
- Winnability verdict (Winnable / Challenging / Hard to Win)
- Key metrics (search volume, difficulty where applicable)
- When it was saved

Use your saved items to:

- **Build your content strategy** — your saved terms define your positioning and saved pillars outline your content plan
- **Compare across research sessions** — run multiple researches and save the best results from each to see them side by side
- **Track what you've committed to** — saved items act as your shortlist of validated opportunities

## Managing saved items

- Remove any item by clicking the save button again
- Saved items persist across research sessions
- All saved items are scoped to the current project

## Research history

Research History is separate from Saved Items. It keeps a log of every research session you've run, organized by type (Terms, Pillars).

Each entry shows:

- The input description used for the research
- When it was run (relative timestamp)
- Processing status if still running

The 3 most recent researches show full expandable details by default. Older researches appear as summary cards — click one to open the full results.
