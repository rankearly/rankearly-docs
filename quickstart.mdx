## Create a project

Every research in RankEarly starts with a project. A project captures your business context so the research modes can generate relevant results.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Projects">
    Open your [dashboard](https://rankearly.pro/project) and click **New Project**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your URL">
    Paste your website URL. This can be your homepage, a specific product page, or a landing page — it doesn't have to be a root domain.

    RankEarly analyzes the page and searches for direct competitors automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review business context">
    RankEarly extracts a structured profile from your site:

    - Product description
    - Target audience
    - Key features and core offering
    - Competitors (name + URL)
    - Business goals

    Review and edit anything that looks off. This context shapes every research you run, so accuracy matters.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm and save">
    RankEarly generates seed phrases from your business context. These seeds are used as starting points for keyword research across all modes.

    Click **Save** to create your project.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Run your first research

With your project set up, pick a research mode based on what you need right now.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Find your core terms">
    **Use Primary Term** when you need to know which terms you can realistically rank for.

    1. Go to your project and open **Terms**
    2. Click **New Research**
    3. RankEarly expands your seeds into ~500 keywords, extracts up to 10 candidate terms, and runs SERP gap analysis on each
    4. Review your results — each term gets a **Build**, **Challenging**, or **Skip** verdict

    The top-ranked winnable term is recommended as your primary term. Use it in landing pages, taglines, and meta titles.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Plan your content">
    **Use Topic Clusters** when you need a full content plan.

    1. Go to your project and open **Pillars**
    2. Click **New Research** and enter a topic or feature you want to cover
    3. RankEarly generates up to 10 content pillars, each with subtopic clusters
    4. Each subtopic represents one article to write, with winnability analysis included

    Save the pillars that matter and start building your content calendar.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Find the right angle">
    **Use Content Angles** when you have an idea but aren't sure how to approach it.

    1. Go to your project and open **Angles**
    2. Click **New Research** and describe your content idea
    3. RankEarly generates 5 different angles, each validated for winnability
    4. Pick the angle with the best combination of relevance and winnability
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
