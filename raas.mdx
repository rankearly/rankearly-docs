Result-as-a-Service is RankEarly's done-for-you blog service. You share what you know about your business, and we turn it into articles you can publish.

AI helps with research and drafting, but a human reviews every piece. You stay involved where your judgment matters: what your business does, what you want to say, and what we should avoid.

<Note>
  Good content depends on good context. If we misunderstand your product, audience, or offer, the article can be wrong even when the writing looks polished.
</Note>

## When to use it

Result-as-a-Service is a good fit when:

- Your site is new and you need real impressions to learn which keywords are worth targeting. Shipping several articles and iterating beats spending days perfecting one.
- Your team is small and most of your time goes into product, launch, sales, and customer work. You want writing done without hiring a full-time content specialist.
- You have useful business insights but do not want to write long SEO articles yourself. You would rather describe what you know and let us turn it into search-ready posts.

If you prefer to run the research and writing workflow yourself, start with the [Quickstart](/quickstart) and use the self-serve platform instead.

## What we need from you

At the start, we ask for the website you want to improve. Usually this is the site where you want better search visibility, clearer positioning, or higher conversion from content.

We read your site, check the sitemap, and collect public information. Then we write up what we think your business does and what the articles should focus on. Before any writing begins, we send those notes back for your review: the business context, topic ideas, and article plans we plan to use. This is where you correct anything we misunderstood.

The part that matters most is your business context:

- What you sell
- Who you serve
- What problems you solve
- What makes your offer different
- Topics, claims, or competitors we should avoid
- Product details, customer language, or proof points we should use

### If your website is thin

If your site is new, thin, or missing important details, we need extra reference material before writing. You have three options.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a public reference page (recommended)">
    Add user-facing documentation or reference pages that explain your product, features, services, use cases, and positioning. These pages help us understand your business, and they also give Google more to read from your site.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share internal docs">
    Send internal notes, sales docs, product docs, onboarding material, or customer research, with confidential data removed. We save the useful parts as context, so we can spot search intents where your product or service can help. Without that context, we may miss good opportunities.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write the context yourself">
    Edit the context document directly. You decide what to share, what to describe, and how your offer should be framed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Public reference pages have a second benefit: they help build topical authority. Sometimes they pick up search queries you were not targeting, and we can use those signals to plan better article topics and turn the search intent into stronger SEO and GEO content.

In general, the more complete your references, the less likely we are to miss product details, positioning, or audience needs. Once the context is approved, we write against that direction.

## Communication

We recommend Slack because it makes feedback faster, especially while we are confirming context or handling revisions. Slack is not required. Email works too.

Timely replies keep the work moving. The replies that matter most are:

- Corrections to business context
- Approvals or changes to topic choices
- Feedback on writing style
- Flagging missing facts before revision work starts

## Revisions

We include three free revision rounds, in case key context was missing or misunderstood. A revision triggered by missing context often means rebuilding the article from the outline onward, followed by another internal review before we send it back.

For small wording preferences or points that come down to personal taste, editing the article yourself is usually faster and gives you more control over the final voice.

We welcome style feedback. When you share preferences, we carry them into future articles.

## What it is not for

Result-as-a-Service is focused on blog content. It is not a replacement for rank tracking, backlink work, technical SEO audits, or a full agency retainer.
