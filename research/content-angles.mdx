Content Angles takes a content idea you already have — a customer question, forum thread, or rough concept — and shows you 5 different ways to write about it. Each angle is validated for winnability so you can pick the approach most likely to rank.

## When to use it

- You have a specific idea but aren't sure how to frame it
- A customer asked a question and you want to turn it into content
- You found a relevant discussion in a forum or community and want to write about it

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Describe your idea">
    Select an input type to give RankEarly context:

    - **Community discussion** — a Reddit thread, forum post, or online conversation
    - **User feedback** — something a user told you about your product
    - **Customer question** — a question someone asked about your space
    - **Content idea** — a rough idea you want to explore

    Then describe the idea in your own words.
  </Step>
  <Step title="AI generates 5 angles">
    RankEarly refines your input into a clear content idea, then generates 5 topics. Each topic is a different way to approach the same core idea.

    Every topic includes:
    - A title (intent-driven, like "How to Choose Waterproof Hiking Shoes")
    - User intent described as a Jobs To Be Done statement
    - 6 keyword variations with search intent
  </Step>
  <Step title="Winnability analysis">
    RankEarly fetches the top 10 Google results for all 30 keywords (5 topics x 6 keywords) and runs [SERP gap analysis](/concepts#winnability) on each.

    Each topic gets a verdict based on how many of its 6 keywords are winnable:

    | Verdict | Condition | Meaning |
    |---|---|---|
    | **Winnable** | 3+ keywords winnable | Strong opportunity across multiple phrasings |
    | **Challenging** | 1-2 keywords winnable | Some phrasings work, but mixed signals |
    | **Hard to Win** | 0 keywords winnable | Tough competition across the board |
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Reading the results

Each of the 5 topics shows:

- **Topic title** — the angle as a content piece (e.g., "Best Budget Waterproof Hiking Shoes for Beginners")
- **Intent type** — Informational, Commercial, or Transactional
- **User intent (JTBD)** — "When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [desired outcome]"
- **Verdict** — Winnable, Challenging, or Hard to Win
- **Winnable count** — e.g., "4/6 keywords viable"
- **Keywords** — 6 keyword variations with search volume, difficulty, and individual verdicts

Click any topic to view the detailed SERP analysis in a slide-over panel.

## What to do with the results

Pick the angle with the best combination of:
1. **Relevance** to your product and audience
2. **Winnability** — favor Winnable verdicts, but Challenging can work if the angle is a strong fit

Save topics to your backlog for writing later. You can run Content Angles multiple times with different ideas to build up a pipeline of validated content opportunities.

## Tips

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Content Angles vs Topic Clusters">
    Use **Content Angles** when you already have an idea and want to find the best approach. Use [Topic Clusters](/research/topic-clusters) when you need to discover what to write about in the first place.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Use real customer language">
    The best input comes from actual customer questions, support tickets, or community discussions. Paste the raw text — RankEarly will clean it up and extract the core idea.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Don't discard Challenging angles">
    A **Challenging** verdict means 1-2 of the 6 keyword phrasings are winnable. If the angle fits your audience well, it can still be worth writing — especially if you target the specific winnable keywords.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Hard to Win means not now">
    A **Hard to Win** verdict means none of the 6 keyword phrasings showed a clear opening. Competition may change over time, so consider revisiting later.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
