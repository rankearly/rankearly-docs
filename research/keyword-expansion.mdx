title: "Keyword expansion"
description: "Turn one content idea into 100-500 keywords for bulk research, pSEO planning, or feeding into external tools."
---

Keyword Expansion generates a large set of keywords from a single content idea. Unlike the other research modes that analyze winnability, Keyword Expansion focuses on **volume** — giving you a comprehensive keyword dataset to work with.

## When to use it

- You need a keyword list for programmatic SEO (pSEO) pages
- You're building a content calendar and want to see the full keyword landscape
- You need raw keyword data to export into external tools or spreadsheets
- You want to compare keyword coverage against competitors

### When not to use it

- **Need to know what to write?** Use [Topic Clusters](/research/topic-clusters) instead
- **Validating a specific idea?** Use [Content Angles](/research/content-angles) instead
- **Finding positioning terms?** Use [Primary Term](/research/primary-term) instead

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Describe your idea">
    Select an input type — community discussion, user feedback, customer question, content idea, feature request, or use case — and describe the idea you want to expand.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a keyword limit">
    Select how many keywords you want:

    | Limit | Credits |
    |---|---|
    | 100 keywords | 15 credits |
    | 200 keywords | 20 credits |
    | 300 keywords (default) | 30 credits |
    | 400 keywords | 40 credits |
    | 500 keywords | 50 credits |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Iterative expansion">
    RankEarly refines your input, generates seed keywords, and fetches related keywords for each seed. If the target hasn't been reached, it generates a new batch of seeds and repeats — up to 9 iterations for 500 keywords.

    Each batch takes about 5-10 seconds. Keywords are deduplicated across all batches.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Results">
    The final keyword list is displayed in a table with four columns:

    - **Keyword** — the keyword text
    - **Search volume** — monthly search volume
    - **KD** — keyword difficulty (0-100)
    - **Intent** — Informational, Commercial, Transactional, or Navigational
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Exporting results

You can export the keyword list as a CSV file. The export includes keyword text only (no metrics), formatted as `keywords-[timestamp].csv`.

Results are static after generation — the table is view-only with no inline editing or filtering.

## Tips

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Start with 300 keywords">
    The default of 300 keywords is a good balance between coverage and cost. Go higher if you need comprehensive coverage for pSEO; go lower for a quick exploration of the keyword landscape.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Feed results into Topic Clusters">
    Keyword Expansion gives you the raw data. If you want those keywords organized into a content plan with pillars and subtopics, follow up with [Topic Clusters](/research/topic-clusters) using a related input.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Use specific inputs">
    The more specific your input, the more relevant the keywords. "AI-powered keyword research tools" produces tighter results than "SEO tools."
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
