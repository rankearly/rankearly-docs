Keyword Expansion is usually the first step in RankEarly research. You start with a topic idea or a list of seed keywords, and RankEarly finds related keywords people actually search for. Everything gets saved into your keyword library — which feeds into [Topic Clusters](/research/topic-clusters) for content planning and [Primary Term](/research/primary-term) for positioning research.

## Two ways to start

### Topic mode (default)

Describe what you want to target in your own words. This can be:

- A content idea you're considering
- A product feature you want to promote
- A use case for your product
- Community discussions or user feedback
- Anything that defines a subject you want to rank for

RankEarly reads your description and generates seed keywords to expand from. The more specific your description, the more focused your results.

**Example:** "Email deliverability tools for cold outreach" produces tighter results than just "email tools."

### Keywords mode

Already know which keywords you want to target? Paste them directly as a comma-separated list. RankEarly expands exactly what you provide — no AI interpretation step.

This is useful when you've already done keyword research elsewhere, have competitor keywords to expand on, or prefer full control over the starting point.

**Example:** Paste "email warmup, cold email software, deliverability testing" and RankEarly finds related keywords for each.

## Search effort levels

Instead of requesting a specific keyword count, you choose how broad the search should be. The effort level determines how many seed keywords RankEarly works from — more seeds mean wider coverage.

| Effort | Seeds | Best For |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Ultra Low | 1 | One specific keyword you want to expand |
| Low | 5 | Niche topics or focused discovery |
| Medium | 10 | Most common topics — a good starting point |
| High | 20 | Broad topics with many angles to explore |
| Ultra High | 30 | Full category research, large-scale pSEO planning |

**Medium is the default** and works well for most topics. Go higher when mapping an entire market. Go lower when you want focused results around a specific term.

### Credit requirements

Each effort level requires a minimum credit balance to start:

| Effort | Min. Credits |
|--------|--------------|
| Ultra Low | 100 |
| Low | 400 |
| Medium | 800 |
| High | 1,500 |
| Ultra High | 2,500 |

If your balance is below an effort's threshold, that option is disabled. Credits are charged after the run completes — not upfront.

## How it works

<img src="https://cdn.rankearly.pro/images/docs/screenshot-keyword-expansion-modal.jpg" alt="Keyword expansion modal where you enter your idea and select search effort" width="500" />

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start with a topic or your own seeds">
    Describe what you want to target in Topic mode, or paste a comma-separated list of keywords in Keywords mode.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your search effort">
    Pick an effort level based on how broad you want the discovery to be. Higher effort means more seeds and wider coverage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="RankEarly finds related keywords">
    For each seed keyword, RankEarly finds related search terms, checks search volume and difficulty, and removes duplicates.
  </Step>
  <Step title="SERP data is collected">
    For every keyword returned, RankEarly captures a snapshot of the current Google search results — so you can see who's ranking.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Everything saves to your library">
    Keywords, seeds, and SERP snapshots are saved into your selected keyword library. Each run adds to the library — run multiple expansions for different topics and the library grows over time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What you get

- A collection of related keywords with search volume, difficulty, and intent data
- SERP snapshots showing who ranks for each keyword
- Exportable keyword lists for spreadsheets or other tools

## Tips

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Start with Keyword Expansion">
    In most workflows, this is the best first step. It populates your keyword library, which other research modes depend on.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Run multiple expansions into the same library">
    Each run adds new keywords to your library. You can run Keyword Expansion multiple times for different topics within the same market — the library grows with each run.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Match effort to your actual need">
    Don't default to the highest effort. If you're researching a niche topic, Low or Medium is usually enough. Save High and Ultra High for when you're mapping a broad category.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
