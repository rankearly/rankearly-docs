---
title: "Keyword expansion"
description: "Turn one content idea into 100-500 keywords, save them into a keyword library, and reuse that data in later research."
---

Keyword Expansion is usually the first step in RankEarly research. It turns one idea into a broad keyword dataset, saves that data into a keyword library, and gives later workflows a reliable base to build on.

That library becomes the foundation for later workflows such as content planning in [Topic Clusters](/research/topic-clusters) and positioning research in [Primary Term](/research/primary-term).

## When to use it

- You're starting research on a new topic, feature, or project
- You want to build or refresh the keyword library for a project
- You need broad keyword coverage for planning, pSEO, or exports
- You want a reusable keyword base for content planning or positioning research

## Keyword targets and credits

Choose how many keywords you want returned:

| Target keywords | Credits |
|---|---|
| 100 | 120 credits |
| 200 | 160 credits |
| 300 | 240 credits |
| 400 | 320 credits |
| 500 | 400 credits |

## How it works

![Keyword expansion modal where you enter your idea and select target keyword count](https://cdn.rankearly.pro/images/docs/screenshot-keyword-expansion-modal.jpg)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start from one idea">
    Give RankEarly a content idea, feature, question, or other source concept you want to expand.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Expand the idea into keywords">
    RankEarly refines the input, generates seed keywords, expands them into related keywords, and deduplicates the results into one dataset.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the results into your library">
    The returned keywords, successful expanded seeds, and SERP snapshots are saved into the selected keyword library.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use the library in later research">
    Once the library is populated, it can power content planning in [Topic Clusters](/research/topic-clusters) or positioning research in [Primary Term](/research/primary-term).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What you get

- A broad keyword set with metrics
- Saved keywords and SERP snapshots inside your keyword library
- A reusable base for content architecture planning and positioning research
- Exportable keyword data for work outside RankEarly

A run can finish below the requested limit if the topic is narrow or too many expansions collapse into duplicates.

## Tips

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Treat Keyword Expansion as the starting point">
    In most workflows, Keyword Expansion is the best first step because it creates the shared keyword library that later research modes depend on.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Use a focused input">
    Specific inputs usually produce tighter keyword sets. A focused idea like `AI keyword clustering for SaaS teams` gives cleaner results than a broad input like `SEO software`.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Choose a larger target only when you need broader coverage">
    Start with `300` for most topics. Move to `400` or `500` when you're mapping a large market, planning pSEO at scale, or building a deeper export set.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
