Primary Term research helps you find the right **positioning terms** — the phrases you use in landing page headlines, taglines, meta titles, and ad copy. Each term gets a winnability verdict so you know which ones are worth targeting.

## When to use it

- You're launching a new site and need to decide how to describe your product
- You want to know which category terms you can realistically compete for
- You need positioning language backed by search data, not guesswork

## How it works

Primary Term research runs in four steps, fully automated after you click **New Research**.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Keyword expansion">
    RankEarly generates ~500 keywords from your project's seed phrases. It starts with 5 AI-generated seeds, fetches related keywords for each, and iterates until it reaches ~500 keywords.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Term extraction">
    AI analyzes the full keyword set and extracts up to 10 candidate terms. Each term is a semantically distinct positioning phrase — modifier variations like "best X" and "top X" are grouped under the same term.

    Each candidate also gets up to 5 **support queries** — related keywords from the dataset that reinforce the term.
  </Step>
  <Step title="SERP gap analysis">
    RankEarly fetches the top 10 Google results for every candidate term and all its support queries, then evaluates six opportunity signals for each:

    - Are small sites or forums ranking?
    - Is Google showing mixed intent?
    - Is there a content format gap?
    - Are results low quality?
    - Are strong competitors dominating?
    - Are SERP features stealing clicks?

    See [winnability](/concepts#winnability) for the full signal breakdown.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verdict">
    Each term gets an overall verdict based on how many of its queries (term + support queries) are winnable:

    | Verdict | Condition | Meaning |
    |---|---|---|
    | **Build** | 3+ winnable queries | Multiple angles reinforce the term — go for it |
    | **Challenging** | 1-2 winnable queries | Some angles viable, but mixed signals |
    | **Skip** | 0 winnable queries | No opportunity in this cluster right now |

    The highest-scoring **Build** term is recommended as your primary term.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Reading the results

### Recommended term

The results page leads with one clear recommendation — the term with the highest winnability score. You'll see:

- The term itself
- **Overall verdict**: Build, Challenging, or Skip
- **Term SERP**: Whether the term's own search results show opportunity
- **Support queries**: How many of the support queries are winnable (e.g., "4/5 support queries viable")
- **Search volume**

### Alternatives

Below the recommendation, you'll find other terms with a **Build** verdict, ranked by winnability score then search volume. These are viable alternatives if the recommended term doesn't fit your positioning.

### All terms

The full list includes Challenging and Skip terms for users who want the complete picture or disagree with the recommendations.

## What to do with your terms

Once you've identified winnable terms, use them in your positioning:

- **Landing page headlines**: "The Best [Term] for [Audience]"
- **Taglines**: "[Term] Built for [Audience]"
- **Meta titles and descriptions** for SEO
- **Ad copy** for paid campaigns
- **Feature page headers** to match search intent

You can save any term to your **Saved Terms** list for reference across research sessions.

## Tips

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Run it more than once">
    Your first research gives you a baseline. As you refine your project context or learn more about your market, run Primary Term again with updated seeds to discover new opportunities.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Don't ignore Challenging terms">
    A **Challenging** verdict means 1-2 support queries are winnable. If the term is a strong fit for your product, it might still be worth targeting — especially if you plan to build authority in that space over time.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Terms and topics work together">
    Your primary term tells you how to position your product. Use [Topic Clusters](/research/topic-clusters) or [Content Angles](/research/content-angles) to plan the content that builds authority around that term.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
