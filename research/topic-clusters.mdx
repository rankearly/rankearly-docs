Topic Clusters surfaces content ideas you haven't thought of yet. You provide a topic or feature, and RankEarly generates up to 10 **content pillars**, each with 5-30 subtopic clusters. Every subtopic represents one article to write.

## When to use it

- You need a full content plan, not just a single article idea
- You want to build topical authority around a theme
- You're starting content planning for a new product feature or vertical

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Keyword expansion">
    RankEarly generates up to 500 keywords from your input using iterative seed generation and keyword API lookups. High keyword difficulty (KD > 70) keywords are filtered out by default — you can disable this.
  </Step>
  <Step title="SERP collection and clustering">
    For each keyword, RankEarly fetches the top 10 Google results. Keywords with 3+ overlapping URLs in their top results are grouped into clusters. Each cluster represents one search intent — one article opportunity.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pillar selection">
    AI selects 10 content pillars from the clusters. Each pillar gets 1-3 **anchor clusters** that define its scope. A pillar is a broad theme (like "Win-Back Campaigns") with enough breadth to support multiple subtopics but enough specificity to stay distinct from other pillars.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Subtopic discovery">
    For each pillar, AI extracts a one-liner subject that defines its focus, then finds 5-30 subtopic clusters that are relevant to that subject. Each subtopic is verified against the pillar's SERP data to filter out mismatches.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Reading the results

Each pillar shows:

- **Pillar name** — the overarching theme (e.g., "Content Writing")
- **Subject** — a one-liner defining the topical focus
- **Subtopics** — 5-30 keyword clusters, each representing one article to write

Each subtopic includes:

- The keywords in the cluster
- Aggregated search volume
- Average keyword difficulty

You can expand any subtopic to view its full keyword list, SERP results, and create a [content brief](/planning/content-brief) directly from it.

## What to do with the results

**Save pillars** you want to pursue. Saved pillars persist across research sessions and retain their full keyword cluster data.

**Plan your content calendar.** Each subtopic is one article. Start with the subtopics that have lower difficulty and higher search volume — these are your quickest wins.

**Build topical authority.** The pillar-cluster structure maps directly to internal linking strategy. Write the pillar page first, then link each subtopic article back to it. This [helps Google understand content relationships](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/links-crawlable).

## Tips

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Filter high-KD keywords">
    The KD filter is on by default. It removes keywords with difficulty above 70, which produces cleaner results for new sites. If you're working with an established site or want the full picture, turn it off.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Run it per feature">
    For products with multiple features or verticals, run Topic Clusters once per feature. This gives you focused pillar structures instead of one large, mixed result.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Topic Clusters vs Content Angles">
    Use **Topic Clusters** when you need ideas — "what should I write about?" Use [Content Angles](/research/content-angles) when you already have an idea and need the best way to approach it — "how should I write about this?"
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
